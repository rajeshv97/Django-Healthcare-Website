Cardiology

Wildcat Healthcare provides a full range of cardiology services, from lifesaving emergency care to managing chronic heart conditions. You have access to the expertise of a leading health network, along with compassionate, personalized care.

Neurology

Providence neurologists provide comprehensive care to patients with neurological disorders that require careful attention. Patients and families affected by conditions like Parkinson's disease, Alzheimer's disease or multiple sclerosis can count on the experience of our compassionate neurologists for their treatment.

Gastroenterology

Our mission is to provide the highest quality, most cost-effective gastroenterology care to our patients. Our facility is dedicated to providing the most up-to-date endoscopy techniques. Our hospital has grown to provide a world-class facility for manometry.

Pediatrics

For over 10 years, parents in the Arizona area have chosen Wildcat Healthcare for their children's health care needs. We provide pediatric care to babies, infants, and adolescents up to 18 years.

Radiology

Around the clock and around the world, we provide radiology reporting 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Our PET/CT reporting services allow Specialty Radiology to provide the highest quality care for our patients.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Who can I contact with questions about my bill?

    If you have questions about your bill, you can speak to a Customer Service representative by calling 866.621.6385 (Monday - Friday; 8 a.m. - 6 p.m. EST), or schedule a call back.

  • What procedures will I have in the Emergency Department?

    Treatments and procedures may include lab work, radiological studies, CAT scans, ultrasound, EKG for cardiac review, medication administration and physician evaluations.

  • How do I pay my bill?

    We accept the following forms of payment and does not charge any processing fees: Check or money order All major credit cards Electronic checks Payroll deduction (Employees only)

  • When will I see a physician?

    All patients are first triaged by an ER nurse. Then patients are evaluated by a physician. Patients who have a higher acuity of illness (i.e. heart attack or stroke) may be treated before those patients with less severe conditions (laceration, sprained ankle, etc.). Your patience is always appreciated.

  • What happens if I am admitted?

    Upon admission to the hospital, you will remain in the Emergency Department until your inpatient room is ready. Your admitting physician will visit you during their designated "patient rounds".
